Global Updates

BANGALORE

IBM Research–India lab here focuses on hybrid cloud, AI, climate/sustainability and quantum computing. Bangalore is also home to IBM’s Infrastructure Development Lab (ISDL), which works on Power systems, mainframes (IBM Z), storage and cloud platforms. In addition, IBM’s consulting, software services and Cloud/Watson teams have large development centers in the city.

KOLKATA

Kolkata is a major IBM delivery and services hub. Historically it has been IBM’s second-largest India workforce after Bangalore. The Kolkata center focuses on application and infrastructure services (e.g. SAP, mainframes, web/app development) and consulting.

DELHI

The Delhi/NCR facilities focus on IBM’s services and research. The Gurgaon research lab works on many of the same domains as Bengaluru (AI, hybrid cloud, quantum). IBM also has sales, consulting and software services teams based in Delhi (for government and enterprise accounts).

Your Erasmus Questions Answered!

What is Erasmus?
+

Erasmus+ is the EU's programme to support education, training, youth and sport in Europe. It has an estimated budget of €26.2 billion and provides opportunities for over 4 million participants to study, train, gain experience, and volunteer abroad.

How to apply for Erasmus?
+

You apply through your home university's international or Erasmus+ office. The application process usually involves submitting an application form, a letter of motivation, and your academic transcripts. Check with your university for specific deadlines and requirements.

What are the benefits?
+

Benefits include gaining international experience, improving language skills, developing intercultural competence, boosting your CV, and building a global network of friends and professional contacts. It's a unique opportunity for personal and academic growth.
